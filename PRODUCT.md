# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

US-based Tech Recruiters, Data Ops Managers, and RevOps Directors scanning candidate portfolios under time pressure. The 5-second filter is real: they need to immediately understand what Fernando does, where he operates, and whether his impact claims hold up under scrutiny. Secondary audience: technical hiring managers verifying depth through case studies and stack specifics.

## Product Purpose

A single-page portfolio that positions Fernando Perez as an outcome-driven Systems & Data Architect. The site must communicate "Time Leverage" (the ability to turn 30-hour manual workflows into 5-minute automated systems) through both its content and its design. Every element demonstrates the same philosophy Fernando applies to his work: deterministic, lean, high signal-to-noise.

## Brand Personality

Precise. Efficient. Unflinching.

Voice is direct and technical without being jargony. Confident without boasting. No hedging, no filler words, no marketing fluff. The copy reads like a well-structured engineering document: facts first, context where needed, zero decoration. The design mirrors this voice through brutalist restraint and deliberate typography.

## Anti-references

- **SaaS landing page templates**: Gradient-heavy hero sections, glassmorphism cards, hero-metric stat grids, blue/purple default palettes, animated illustrations, trust logos.
- **Generic devfolio tropes**: Terminal-green-on-black color schemes, monospace-as-personality, animated typing cursors, fake terminal windows.
- **Agency portfolios**: Scroll-jacking, heavy animation, 3D hero objects, over-designed flourishes, pretentious design language.
- **Any pattern** where the design is working harder than the content.

## Design Principles

1. **Signal over noise.** Every element must earn its place. If it doesn't communicate value to a recruiter making a 5-second scan, it gets cut. No decoration, no filler, no "design for design's sake."

2. **Deterministic precision.** The layout, spacing, and typography should feel engineered, not styled. Deliberate choices visible at a glance. The page should communicate "systems thinking" before a single word is read.

3. **Time leverage as narrative.** The structure itself demonstrates efficiency. Content hierarchy minimizes cognitive load. The page loads fast, reads fast, and respects the user's time. Practice what the case studies preach.

4. **Expert confidence.** No hedging, no over-explaining, no apologetic tone. Present facts and outcomes. Let the work speak. The design should feel like it was made by someone who knows exactly what they're doing.

5. **Lean execution.** Demonstrate "mucho con poco" through performance. Zero unnecessary dependencies. Static export. The site should feel fast in a way that hiring managers notice.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA compliance minimum. All content must pass contrast requirements. Semantic HTML throughout. Keyboard-navigable. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. The brutalist aesthetic must not compromise readability or usability.
