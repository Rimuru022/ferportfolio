# Design System: Fernando Perez Portfolio

## 1. Overview

**Creative North Star: "The Precision Instrument"**

This design system treats the portfolio as an engineered object: every measurement deliberate, every surface functional, every word load-bearing. The aesthetic philosophy is brutalist minimalism stripped of decoration. Layout communicates systems thinking before a single word is read. Typography is invisible in the best way: it carries information without calling attention to itself.

The surface is light, but never clinical. Warmth and subtlety prevent the harsh glare of pure white-on-black contrast. The overall mood is calm authority: a well-lit workshop, not a hospital corridor.

The system rejects anything that looks like it was generated by a template. No gradients, no glassmorphism, no decorative animation, no hero metric grids. The page should feel like it was built by someone who optimizes cloud infrastructure for a living: lean, secure, and highly leveraged.

**Key Characteristics:**
- Warm light surface, warm dark text, muted cobalt accent.
- Single technical sans-serif family, weight-driven hierarchy.
- Flat surfaces, zero drop shadows at rest.
- Sharp or near-sharp corners; no excessive rounding.
- State changes only for motion; no entrance choreography.
- Muted, eye-friendly tones throughout. No pure white or pure black.

## 2. Colors

The palette is restrained: warm tinted neutrals with a single disciplined, desaturated accent. The accent is rare by design; its scarcity is the point. Every color is chosen to reduce eye strain under typical office ambient light.

### Primary
- **Muted Cobalt Signal** [to be resolved during implementation]: The sole accent. A desaturated, medium-lightness cobalt. Used for interactive states, key highlights, and the rare moment of emphasis. Appears on ≤10% of any given screen. Never saturated or electric.

### Neutral
- **Warm Ivory** [to be resolved during implementation]: Primary background. A gentle off-white tinted toward warm gray. Eliminates the harshness of #fff under ambient light.
- **Warm Charcoal** [to be resolved during implementation]: Primary text. A near-black with warmth, not a stark #000. Easier on the eyes over long reading sessions.
- **Soft Ash** [to be resolved during implementation]: Secondary text, borders, dividers, metadata. A mid-gray with warmth, providing hierarchy without drawing attention.
- **Warm Surface** [to be resolved during implementation]: Alternate background for section separation. Slightly darker than Warm Ivory, used sparingly to create tonal depth without shadows.

**The One Accent Rule.** The primary accent color is used on ≤10% of any given screen. Its rarity is the point. Do not use it for decoration, borders, or background fills.

**The No-Pure-Extremes Rule.** Never use #000 or #fff. Tint every neutral toward the warm or cobalt hue family. Pure extremes cause eye strain and feel cheap.

## 3. Typography

**Display/Body Font:** [Single technical sans-serif to be chosen at implementation, e.g., Inter, Geist, or Space Grotesk]

**Character:** Technical, neutral, confident. The typeface does not perform personality; it delivers information with zero friction. Hierarchy is established through weight contrast and scale, not color or decoration.

### Hierarchy
- **Display** (bold, clamp(2.5rem, 6vw, 4rem), 1.05): Hero headline only. Tight leading for impact.
- **Headline** (semibold, 1.5rem–2rem, 1.2): Section titles.
- **Title** (medium, 1.125rem–1.25rem, 1.3): Subsection headings, case study titles.
- **Body** (regular, 1rem, 1.6): Paragraphs. Capped at 70ch max line length.
- **Label** (medium, 0.75rem–0.875rem, 1.4, uppercase, letter-spacing: 0.05em): Tags, metadata, timestamps, category labels.

**The Weight Contrast Rule.** Hierarchy lives in weight and scale, not in color switches. Avoid using the accent color for headings. Let the typeface's weight range do the work.

## 4. Elevation

This system is flat. Surfaces sit on the same plane; depth is communicated exclusively through tonal contrast (background vs. text) and spacing, never through shadows.

There is no shadow vocabulary. Do not use box-shadow for cards, buttons, or containers.

**The Flat-By-Default Rule.** Surfaces are flat at rest. If an element needs to feel elevated, use tonal layering (a slightly different background shade) or spacing, not blur or shadow.

## 6. Do's and Don'ts

### Do:
- **Do** use a single sans-serif family for the entire page. Let weight and scale create hierarchy, not font switching.
- **Do** cap body text at 70ch line length.
- **Do** use the accent color sparingly: interactive states, the hero name, and one key highlight per section maximum.
- **Do** respect `prefers-reduced-motion`. Any state transitions should be instant or near-instant for users who request reduced motion.
- **Do** vary spacing rhythmically. Not every section needs the same padding.
- **Do** tint all neutrals warm. Warm Ivory backgrounds, Warm Charcoal text. Avoid pure white and pure black.
- **Do** desaturate the cobalt accent. It should feel like a calm signal, not a warning light.

### Don't:
- **Don't** use gradient text, gradient backgrounds, or any gradients whatsoever.
- **Don't** use border-left or border-right greater than 1px as a colored accent on cards, list items, or callouts.
- **Don't** use glassmorphism, backdrop-filter blur, or any translucent layered surfaces.
- **Don't** use the hero-metric template (big number, small label, supporting stats, gradient accent).
- **Don't** use identical card grids with icon + heading + text repeated endlessly.
- **Don't** use side-stripe borders as accent marks.
- **Don't** use em dashes anywhere in copy.
- **Don't** animate layout properties (width, height, top, left). If a transition exists, it must use transform or opacity only.
- **Don't** use a typical SaaS landing-page palette or structure (gradient-heavy hero sections, hero-metric stat grids, animated illustrations, trust logos).
- **Don't** use generic devfolio tropes (terminal-green-on-black, monospace-as-personality, animated typing cursors, fake terminal windows).
- **Don't** use agency portfolio patterns (scroll-jacking, heavy animation, 3D hero objects, over-designed flourishes).
- **Don't** use pure #000 or #fff anywhere. These cause eye strain and feel unfinished.
